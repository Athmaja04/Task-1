Heart Attack Assistance

Recognize the signs of a heart attack:

  1. Chest pain or discomfort, often described as pressure, squeezing, or fullness
  2. Pain or discomfort in the upper body, including arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
  3. Shortness of breath
  4. Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness
What to Do:

What to do if you suspect someone is having a heart attack:

  1. Call emergency services immediately.
  2. If the person is conscious, have them chew and swallow an aspirin.
  3. Keep the person calm and seated while waiting for help to arrive.
  4. Encourage deep breathing to help reduce stress and anxiety.
  5. If the person becomes unresponsive and stops breathing normally, begin CPR .
